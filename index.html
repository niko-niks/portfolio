About

My journey began when I was still a kid when I got curious about technology. It started with troubleshooting hardware (I only have a phone at the time). I have an insatiable hunger for knowledge. I used to tinker with my phone a lot to be able to use it to its full potential. Due to my interest in hardware tinkering, someone eventually noticed what I'm doing and asked me to volunteer for them. It was fun. I liked the routine of checking their hardware to make sure they will run smoothly. Then the COVID pandemic came and I pursued the Computer System Servicing from TESDA online. It basically teaches anyone about computer hardware and also how to build it. It was very helpful for me and I actually got to use it on my own computer when I upgraded it for my learning needs while I'm pursuing my degree in Computer Science as well as for my hobby like online games.

Regarding my education, it is fun to learn how to build websites and applications as long as I can fix the problems that comes along with it. I believe it is quite fun as long as you never get tired to troubleshoot when encountering problems and also when you never lose the desire to keep learning more and new things. There is power in being able to build websites and applications when I only used to be the consumer or user of such websites and applications.

As for my future plans, I want to work in a cybersecurity field one day. It seems fun. I am actually learning about ethical hacking as of the moment along with my studies. I got a glimpse of what cybsersecurity was about in one of my subjects wherein we analyzed packets with Wireshark and learned about networks.
